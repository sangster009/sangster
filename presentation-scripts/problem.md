# Problem Deep Dive

**Page:** `problem.html`  
**Title:** Problem deepdive — Attribution Reporting

---

## Script

- **Opening:** "Here we structure the **problem** into three areas. Expand the accordions as we go."

- **Operational inefficiency:** "Customers contact sales to create custom reports. Sales manually assembles campaign insights. Turnaround is slow and expensive. That’s the operational side."

- **Cognitive overload:** "Too many metrics, and no guidance on what matters most, what’s underperforming, or what to optimize. Users are left to figure it out themselves."

- **Insights:** "The product doesn’t **translate data into decisions**. Users need **prioritization**, not just dashboards. The company is **subsidizing product gaps with human labor**. There’s a clear opportunity to shift from **reporting tool to AI performance partner**. The real customer job is: **Help me improve campaign performance with confidence.**"

- **Summary:** "So we framed the solution around: close the interpretation gap, close the decision gap, and reduce sales dependency. That’s what the AI-first design addresses."

- **Transition:** "Next is **Why we built this**—the product and business goals—then **Design iterations** and **Design details**."

---

## Talking points

1. Operational: sales-dependent, manual reports, slow and expensive.
2. Cognitive: too many metrics, no prioritization or guidance.
3. Insights: translate data → decisions; prioritization over dashboards; reporting tool → AI performance partner.
4. Customer job: improve campaign performance with confidence.
