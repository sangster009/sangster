# Current Workflow

**Page:** `current-workflow.html`  
**Title:** Current Workflow — Campaign reporting experience

---

## Script

- **Opening:** "This is the **current** campaign reporting experience—before the redesign. I'll walk through it; you can click the step boxes to expand and see pain points where we've marked them."

- **First row:** "User has a campaign that needs review. They log in, see the dashboard, then hit a campaign report with **40+ metrics**—no summary, no prioritization, cognitive overload. Then manual filtering and drilldowns: time-consuming, easy to get lost, no guided exploration. They click into charts, export data—and run into data rabbit holes, manual interpretation, and no recommendations."

- **Second row (Show what happens next):** "They try to create a report, copy data into slides—time-consuming, risk of misinterpretation, no insight narrative. They get frustrated, contact Foursquare sales, sales creates a custom report manually—internal inefficiency, slow turnaround, high cost—and the customer finally receives the report, exhausted."

- **Summary:** "So the current state is: dashboard-first, raw metrics, manual analysis, and heavy sales dependency. That’s what we’re solving."

- **Transition:** "Next we’ll look at **customer feedback**—what users actually said—then an expert UX review and a structured problem deep dive."

---

## Talking points

1. Current flow: login → dashboard → 40+ metrics, no summary → manual drilldowns → export → copy to slides → frustration → contact sales → custom report.
2. Pains: no prioritization, cognitive overload, no recommendations, sales dependency, slow turnaround.
3. Sets up customer feedback and problem framing.
