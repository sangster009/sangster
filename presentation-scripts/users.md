# Overview (Who it's for)

**Page:** `users.html`  
**Title:** Who it's for — Attribution Reporting

---

## Script

- **Opening:** "This page summarizes who we're designing for and why. Use the carousel or arrows to move through the cards."

- **Slide 1 — Who it's for:** "Agencies and advertisers who need to interpret campaign performance and share insights with clients. They're the primary users of the reporting experience."

- **Slide 2 — Core problems:** "Their pain: too many metrics, no prioritization, manual report creation, and dependency on sales for custom insights. That’s what we had to fix."

- **Slide 3 — Business impact:** "Impact today: slow turnaround, cognitive overload, and lost confidence when data isn’t translated into clear next steps."

- **Slide 4 — Project snapshot:** "My role was Principal Product Designer slash PM—end-to-end ownership across the reporting lifecycle. This gives you the scope before we go into the ad tech flow and current workflow."

- **Transition:** "Next we’ll look at the Ad Tech flow—who does what from campaign setup to measurement—then the current workflow and its pain points."

---

## Talking points

1. Who: agencies/advertisers interpreting performance and sharing insights.
2. Problems: metrics overload, manual reports, sales dependency.
3. Impact: slow turnaround, overload, low confidence.
4. Role: Principal Product Designer / PM, end-to-end reporting.
