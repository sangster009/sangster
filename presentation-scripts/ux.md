# Design Details

**Page:** `ux.html`  
**Title:** Design Details

---

## Script

- **Opening:** "This page is **Design details**—the promise to the user and the structure of the experience. Expand the accordions as we go."

- **Why AI-first?** "AI-first turns an analytics tool into an **intelligent assistant** that explains what happened and what to do next. We translate data into insights, turn insights into actions, make advanced analysis accessible with natural language and simpler workflows, and personalize what we show—marketers get campaign performance, executives get business impact, analysts get deeper metrics."

- **UX contract:** "Our promise: transform reactive reporting into an **AI-first intelligence platform** so agencies can self-serve campaign insights in minutes. The assistant summarizes performance, detects trends and anomalies, identifies drivers, and generates plain-English insights. It does *not* guarantee causation, guess beyond the data, auto-change campaigns without approval, or replace human strategy. **AI suggests; human approves;** automation requires explicit opt-in. If data is incomplete we show a warning; if the model is uncertain we ask the user. That’s the contract."

- **Design details section:** "Below that we have **Information architecture**—campaign and reporting structure—**Design system** (e.g. shadcn), **Page anatomy**—how the layout supports reading order and a focus mode for the AI—and **Design details** for left nav, control panel, report generation, prompt input, and misc. You can switch tabs to explore each."

- **Transition:** "Next we’ll look at **Workflow**—current vs future, step-by-step—then **Touchpoints** (what changed) and **Final design** with the hero video and design details."

---

## Talking points

1. Why AI-first: intelligent assistant, data → insights → actions, accessibility, personalization.
2. UX contract: promise, what it does/doesn’t do, who decides, failure handling.
3. IA, design system, page anatomy, micro-interactions.
4. Sets up workflow comparison and final design.
